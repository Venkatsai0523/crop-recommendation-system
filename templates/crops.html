Crop Information 🌾

🌱 Rice

Seasonal Conditions: High humidity, warm climate

Rice (Oryza sativa) is a staple food crop grown mainly during the Kharif season (June–November), though it can also be cultivated in the Rabi and Zaid seasons in certain regions. It thrives in a warm and humid climate, requiring temperatures between 20°C to 35°C and high rainfall (100-200 cm) or controlled irrigation. The crop grows best in clayey loam or alluvial soil with good water retention and a pH range of 5.5 to 7.0. Rice cultivation demands standing water (5-10 cm depth) during most of its growth stages, making it highly dependent on irrigation in non-monsoon areas. Harvesting typically takes place 3-6 months after sowing, depending on the variety. Proper fertilizer management is crucial for better yield and quality. Temperature: 20°C - 35°C

Soil Content: High organic content

Soil Type: Clayey soil

🌽 Maize

Seasonal Conditions: Warm and sunny

Maize (Zea mays), also known as corn, is a widely grown cereal crop that thrives in warm climates with temperatures between 21°C to 30°C. It requires moderate rainfall (50-100 cm) and well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil with a pH range of 5.5 to 7.0. Maize is cultivated in both Kharif and Rabi seasons, depending on regional climate conditions. It is highly water-sensitive, requiring proper irrigation during flowering and grain-filling stages. The crop typically matures within 90-120 days after sowing.

Temperature: 18°C - 27°C

Soil Content: Rich in nitrogen & phosphorus

Soil Type: Well-drained loamy soil

🧵 Jute

Seasonal Conditions: High humidity, warm temperatures

Jute (Corchorus spp.) is a fiber crop primarily grown during the Kharif season in warm, humid climates. It requires temperatures between 24°C to 37°C and high rainfall (150-200 cm). Jute thrives in well-drained alluvial or loamy soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5. The crop is mainly cultivated in river basin areas, as it needs continuous moisture during its growth period. Harvesting takes place around 100-120 days after sowing, and fibers are extracted through a process called retting.

Temperature: 25°C - 35°C

Soil Content: Rich in organic matter

Soil Type: Sandy loam
